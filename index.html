🧠 Clinical Case

Mrs. C, 49 years old with epigastric pain & vomiting.

CT Scan demonstrated herniation through esophageal hiatus.

📘 Definition
Hiatus hernia is herniation of abdominal contents into thoracic cavity through diaphragm.
📊 Epidemiology
Prevalence increases with age (10% → 50% in elderly).
⚠️ Clinical Presentation
Epigastric pain, fullness, nausea, vomiting. Often incidental.
🧩 Types
Type 1: Sliding (95%)
Type 2: Paraesophageal
Type 3: Mixed
Type 4: With other viscera
🩻 CT Features
- Stomach above diaphragm
- Widened hiatus (>15mm)
💊 Management
Symptomatic → Surgery
Asymptomatic → Conservative
